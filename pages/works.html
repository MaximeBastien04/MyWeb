Journalist Horror Game Demo (WIP)

17/10/2024

✖

Journalist Horror Game Demo (WIP)

In this project, I focused on developing gameplay mechanics and interactive elements for a survival-horror puzzle game where players explore a dark, mysterious environment, solve puzzles, and avoid a lurking monster. My role involved coding, while a friend from Howest created all the 3D assets.

Key Research & Implementation

  • Animations: Completed furniture animations and integrated sound effects for all interactive objects, enhancing realism.
  • Particle System: Implemented a dust particle system using a tutorial to amplify immersion in the abandoned house setting. Adjusted particles dynamically based on player actions—particles increase in size and speed when the player walks or runs, simulating dust clouds.
  • Post-Processing: Applied post-processing profiles to heighten the eerie atmosphere.
  • Sound Integration: Learned to sync audio with animations using animation events, allowing precise timing for multiple audio clips.

Key Challenges & Solutions

  • Dynamic Particle Adjustments: Initially, I hadn't planned for particle changes through code. By examining the PlayerScript, I modified the particles to react based on player movements, creating a more interactive environment.
  • Sound Effects for Animations: Adding sound to animations proved challenging due to multiple audio clips. I resolved this by incorporating animation events and tracking object states with boolean logic, ensuring accurate audio playback.

Results:

The combination of post-processing, particle effects, and sound design significantly enhances the game's immersion. This sprint deepened my understanding of integrating audio and particles into interactive elements, crucial for creating a believable survival-horror experience.

Tools Used: Unity, Particle System, Animation Events, Post-Processing, C#

2D Unity Testing

These sprints improved my problem-solving and research skills, helping me tackle complex challenges through iterative prototypes. The results are crucial for my final work and future game development projects.

Final Work Test

21/10/2024

✖

Final Work Test

During this sprint, I focused on developing the visual style and creating interactive assets for my final work in Unity, refining both character design and environment elements.

Key Research & Implementation

  • Visual Style & Asset Creation: Designed characters and environment assets in Illustrator, then recreated a scene in Unity to ensure stylistic consistency.
  • Character Movement & Lighting: Implemented horizontal player movement and experimented with 2D lighting using Unity's URP. I made the lighting interactive, allowing players to toggle lights via a switch.
  • Inverse Kinematics & Animation: Learned and applied inverse kinematics for smoother animations, resulting in idle and walking animations.

Challenges & Solutions

  • URP Lighting Confusion: Some URP file names differed due to an outdated tutorial. I resolved this through trial and error until the correct files were identified.
  • Walking Animation Difficulty: Creating fluid movement was challenging, so I referenced frame-by-frame animation cycles to improve accuracy.

Results

This sprint established a solid foundation for my final work by defining the game's visual identity and enhancing interactive elements. Inverse kinematics simplified future animation tasks, particularly for item interactions.

Tools Used: Unity, Adobe Illustrator

3D Modeling & Animation || Museum fight

For my 3D modeling course, I created a fully animated scene featuring Bender from Futurama. I began by modeling Bender from a character sheet I found online, then rigged and skinned him in Maya. The texturing process was done using Adobe Substance 3D Painter, bringing Bender's metallic look to life.

This assignment taught me the fundamentals of 3D modeling, rigging, and skinning in Maya, as well as how to integrate animations into Unity—skills I now apply in my game development projects.
